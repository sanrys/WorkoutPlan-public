**Goals:** Muscle Building + Strength + Fat Loss
**HIIT:** 3 sessions/week (45-min Equinox class post-weights on Push days)

## HIIT Options (Push Days: Sun/Tue/Thu)

**Primary:** 45-min Equinox HIIT class (always done post-weights)

**Backup options** if Equinox class isn't available that day:

| Option | Protocol | Duration |
|--------|----------|----------|
| **Assault Bike Intervals** | 20s sprint / 40s recovery x 15 rounds, rest 2 min, repeat | 30-40 min |
| **Rowing Sprints** | 500m all-out / 90s recovery x 10 rounds | 30-35 min |
| **Battle Ropes + Box Jumps + KB Swings** | 40s each, 30s rest, rotate x 8 rounds | 35-40 min |
| **Kettlebell Complex** | 10 swings, 5 cleans, 5 snatches/arm, 60s rest x 8 rounds | 35-40 min |

> **Why post-weights?** Strength and hypertrophy require maximum neural drive and fresh muscles. 45 min of HIIT beforehand would significantly reduce force output on compounds like squats and bench press. Doing HIIT after lifting preserves your strength performance where it matters most, and the HIIT still gets the fat-burning benefit of training in a glycogen-depleted state.

## Progressive Overload Strategy

Use **double progression** -- hit the top of the rep range with good form across all sets before adding weight:

| Day Type | Rep Scheme | Progression Rule |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|
| **A days (Strength)** | 4-6 reps | Add 2.5-5 lbs when you hit all reps cleanly |
| **B days (Hypertrophy)** | 8-12 reps | Add weight when you hit top rep target on all sets |
| **C days (Power)** | 4-6 reps | Focus on bar speed; add weight every 2-3 weeks |

**Deload:** Every 4-6 weeks, reduce volume by 40% while keeping intensity. Listen to your body -- if performance drops for 2+ sessions, deload early.

## Nutrient Timing: Carbs

Carbs are your primary fuel for both lifting and HIIT. Timing them around training makes a real difference for performance and recovery.

### Pre-Workout (60-90 min before lifting)

This is your **most important carb meal of the day**. You need glycogen loaded before you touch a barbell.

| What | Amount | Examples |
|------|--------|----------|
| Complex carbs | 40-60g | Oatmeal, rice, sweet potato, whole grain toast |
| Protein | 25-35g | Eggs, chicken, Greek yogurt, whey shake |
| Fats | Keep low | Fat slows digestion -- save it for other meals |

> **Why pre-workout carbs matter:** Glycogen is the primary fuel for heavy lifting (anaerobic). Walking into a squat session under-fueled means lower force output, earlier fatigue, and worse performance. On HIIT days this is even more critical since you're doing 45 min of high-intensity work after lifting.

If you train early and can't eat a full meal 90 min out, a quick option 30 min before:
- Banana + rice cake + small whey shake
- White bread with honey + protein shake

### Intra-Workout (HIIT days only)

On your 3 HIIT days (Sun/Tue/Thu), sessions run long (~90 min total). Sipping on fast carbs during the HIIT portion can help sustain intensity.

- 20-30g fast carbs in water (Gatorade, highly branched cyclic dextrin, or coconut water)
- Optional but helpful if you notice energy crashing in the second half of HIIT class

### Post-Workout (within 60 min after training)

This is your **second priority carb meal**. The goal is replenishing glycogen and spiking insulin to shuttle protein into muscle.

| What | Amount | Examples |
|------|--------|----------|
| Fast/moderate carbs | 50-80g | White rice, potatoes, pasta, rice cakes, fruit |
| Protein | 30-50g | Chicken breast, whey shake, fish, lean beef |
| Fats | Keep moderate | Some fat is fine here, don't stress over it |

> **HIIT days vs lifting-only days:** On HIIT days you've burned significantly more glycogen. Go toward the higher end (70-80g carbs post-workout). On lifting-only Pull days, 50-60g is sufficient.

### Rest of the Day

- Distribute remaining carbs across other meals -- no need to go low-carb outside the training window
- Include carbs at dinner for better sleep quality (carbs increase serotonin/melatonin production)
- **Saturday (rest day):** You can reduce total carbs slightly (drop 20-30%) since energy demand is lower, but don't go zero-carb -- you're recovering for Sunday's session

## Nutrient Timing: Protein

Protein timing is less critical than carb timing, but distribution across the day matters more than any single meal.

### The Key Rule: 4-5 meals, 30-50g protein each

Muscle protein synthesis (MPS) peaks and resets roughly every 3-4 hours. Eating 150g of protein in 2 meals is less effective than spreading it across 4-5 feedings.

| Meal | Timing | Protein Target | Examples |
|------|--------|---------------|----------|
| **Meal 1** | Morning / wake up | 30-40g | Eggs + egg whites, Greek yogurt, protein oats |
| **Meal 2 (pre-workout)** | 60-90 min before training | 25-35g | Chicken + rice, lean beef + sweet potato |
| **Meal 3 (post-workout)** | Within 60 min after training | 30-50g | Whey shake + banana, chicken + white rice |
| **Meal 4** | Afternoon / evening | 30-40g | Fish, lean meat, tofu + grain bowl |
| **Meal 5 (before bed)** | 30-60 min before sleep | 30-40g | Casein shake, cottage cheese, Greek yogurt |

### Pre-Workout Protein

- Include 25-35g in your pre-workout meal
- Whey or whole food protein -- both work
- This ensures amino acids are circulating during training

### Post-Workout Protein

- 30-50g within 60 min after training
- Whey is ideal here because of fast absorption, but a whole food meal works too
- The "anabolic window" isn't as narrow as bro-science claims, but there's no reason to delay either

### Before Bed

- **Casein protein** (slow-digesting) is the best choice before sleep
- 30-40g of casein or cottage cheese provides a sustained amino acid release for 6-8 hours overnight
- This is especially valuable on HIIT days when total recovery demand is highest
